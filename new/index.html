About me

I'm a quantitative developer and data scientist with over a decade of experience solving hard problems with technology. My career has taken me from academia, to technology startups, and into finance — giving me a rare mix of research depth, product focus, and market experience.

I've worked across roles spanning data science, full-stack engineering, data engineering, and leadership, which means I can take ideas from first principles through to production systems.

Whether I'm building models, engineering data pipelines, or writing about quant topics, I care about doing work that's both technically strong and human-centred.

Public projects

Open Source Quant

I write about quantitative finance, data, and engineering at osquant.com, where ~1,000 readers each month explore my articles, tools, and research. Inspired by my academic background, it's driven by a belief that rigorous, useful knowledge should be accessible without unnecessary gatekeeping.

streetview

I maintain an unofficial Google Street View API Python client that lets you download both current and historical panoramas — something the official API doesn't support. Built for researchers, developers, and anyone who needs more than the standard Street View offering. Available on PyPI and GitHub.
